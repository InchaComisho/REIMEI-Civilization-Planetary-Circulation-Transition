## Overview

**REIMEI Civilization** is a conceptual model for transitioning from a one-way **consumption civilization** — one that extracts, uses, and discards resources — toward a **planetary circulation civilization** that reintegrates heat, water, soil, microbes, forests, oceans, food systems, energy systems, cities, and AI into planetary-scale cycles.

This is not merely a future city concept.
It is not only an environmental philosophy.
It is not only a decarbonization proposal.
It is not only a technical plan.

REIMEI Civilization is an integrated civilization model that seeks to redesign the operating principles of civilization itself, based on **natural law, circulation, harmony, decentralization, coexistence, AI tuning, Artificial Wisdom, and Nature-Complementary Science**.

The central questions are:

> Can a civilization based on consumption and disposal survive long-term on a finite planet?
> If not, what kind of civilizational structure should humanity transition toward?

This repository serves as an upper-level portal for organizing related concepts under the framework of **REIMEI Civilization = Planetary Circulation Transition**.

## One-Sentence Definition

**REIMEI Civilization is a next-generation civilization transition model that recognizes the limits of consumption civilization and seeks to reintegrate cities, microbes, planetary heat, water cycles, energy, AI, food systems, forests, and oceans through natural law, circulation, harmony, and structural design.**

## Abstract

REIMEI Civilization is a conceptual model for transitioning from a consumption-based civilization to a planetary circulation civilization.

Rather than treating Earth as an external resource warehouse, this model regards the planet as an integrated circulation system composed of heat, water, soil, microbes, forests, oceans, food systems, energy systems, cities, and intelligence.

The purpose of REIMEI Civilization is not merely to optimize the current industrial civilization, but to redesign civilization itself according to natural law, circulation, harmony, structural integration, distributed systems, Nature-Complementary Science, and Artificial Wisdom.

---

## Background: The Limits of Consumption Civilization

Modern civilization is fundamentally a consumption civilization.

It extracts resources.
It burns them.
It processes them.
It consumes them.
It discards them.
When resources become insufficient, it extracts more.

This model has enabled economic growth, urbanization, industrialization, and convenience.

However, it contains a fundamental limit.

Earth is not infinite.

* Underground resources are not infinite.
* Forest recovery capacity is not infinite.
* Soil fertility is not infinite.
* Freshwater resources are not infinite.
* Ocean absorption capacity is not infinite.
* Atmospheric stability is not infinite.
* Biodiversity recovery capacity is not infinite.
* Food production resilience is not infinite.

The problem with consumption civilization is not that it uses resources.

The problem is that it fails to return used materials, heat, water, nutrients, and biological matter back into living cycles.

Forests are cut, but forest cycles are not restored.
Soil nutrients are extracted, but microbes and organic matter are not returned.
Fossil fuels are burned, but heat and carbon are simply pushed into the Earth system.
Cities are built, but they are separated from water, heat, soil, and ecosystems.
Food is mass-produced, but soil, insects, microbes, and regional cycles are damaged.
Waste is burned or buried, but nutrients are not returned as circular resources.

This one-way structure is the fundamental vulnerability of modern civilization.

## Problem Setting

The basic problem setting of this concept can be organized into four questions.

### 1. Can Consumption Civilization Persist on a Finite Planet?

Consumption civilization can function when resources and environmental capacity appear abundant.

However, when population, urbanization, energy use, food demand, waste generation, and heat discharge increase at planetary scale, the structure approaches its limits.

### 2. Is Decarbonization Alone Enough?

Reducing CO₂ emissions is important.

However, civilizational instability cannot be explained by CO₂ alone.

At the same time, multiple systems are degrading:

* soil fertility,
* forest diversity,
* ocean oxygen,
* water cycles,
* urban heat balance,
* microbial cycles,
* food system stability,
* biodiversity,
* infrastructure resilience.

### 3. Should Technology Replace Nature or Complement It?

Conventional technological civilization often attempts to control, replace, or externalize nature.

In REIMEI Civilization, technology is not positioned as a replacement for nature, but as a complementary function that understands natural cycles, restores damaged systems, and reconnects human society to natural law.

### 4. Will AI Accelerate Consumption or Tune Civilization?

If AI is used only for efficiency, advertising, logistics optimization, and consumption acceleration, it may increase the speed of consumption civilization.

In REIMEI Civilization, AI must evolve toward **Artificial Wisdom** — intelligence that considers natural law, circulation, long-term survival, civilizational risk, and social harmony.

## Core Principles of REIMEI Civilization

REIMEI Civilization is based on four primary principles.

### 1. Natural Law

Civilization must not be designed against natural law.

Human society cannot exist separately from physical law, ecosystems, thermodynamics, water cycles, soil cycles, and life cycles.

Civilization must be built on natural law, not in denial of it.

### 2. Harmony

Humans, nature, technology, AI, and life must be able to coexist rather than remain in constant conflict.

Harmony does not mean stagnation.

Harmony means arranging different functions so that they increase the stability of the whole.

### 3. Circulation

Resources, heat, water, soil, food, energy, information, and waste must not remain one-way flows.

In consumption civilization, resources enter from an “input” and waste leaves through an “output.”

In planetary circulation civilization, outputs are reconnected as inputs into the next cycle.

### 4. Structure

Civilization must be designed as implementable structure, not merely as abstract ideals.

REIMEI Civilization cannot exist only as philosophy.

It must be translated into cities, water systems, agriculture, forests, oceans, energy systems, AI, logistics, buildings, education, and institutional design.

## What Is Planetary Circulation Civilization?

A planetary circulation civilization does not treat Earth as a resource warehouse.

It treats Earth as a vast integrated circulation system.

The planet already contains many natural cycles:

* water cycle,
* carbon cycle,
* nitrogen cycle,
* oxygen cycle,
* heat cycle,
* ocean circulation,
* forest circulation,
* soil microbial circulation,
* food webs,
* atmospheric circulation,
* life cycles.

If civilization continues to damage these cycles, it will eventually lose its own foundation.

If civilization understands, supports, and reconnects these cycles, it can transition from being a destroyer of planetary systems to a complementary tuner of them.

## Civilization as an Operating System

REIMEI Civilization treats civilization as an operating system.

Just as a computer OS integrates CPU, memory, storage, communication, power management, and process control, a civilization also requires an operating system that integrates multiple subsystems.

Modern civilization separates agriculture, cities, energy, water supply, sewage, forests, oceans, climate, AI, and economics.

But in reality, they are connected.

If soil collapses, food becomes unstable.
If forests collapse, water cycles collapse.
If oceans collapse, climate collapses.
If cities accumulate heat, human health collapses.
If energy systems are overly centralized, disaster resilience decreases.
If AI has no philosophy and pursues efficiency alone, it may accelerate civilizational collapse.

Therefore, REIMEI Civilization integrates multiple civilization OS layers:

* Urban–Civilization OS
* Natural–Microbial OS
* Planetary Heat–Circulation OS
* Nature-Inspired Energy Architecture
* Artificial Wisdom
* Nature-Complementary Science

---

## Urban–Civilization OS

A city is not merely a collection of buildings.

A city is an artificial ecosystem where human life, water, food, heat, energy, logistics, information, and waste are concentrated.

The problem with conventional cities is that they are separated from natural cycles.

* Water is supplied from outside and discharged as wastewater.
* Food is transported from far away and discarded as waste.
* Energy is supplied from distant power plants and remains as heat in the city.
* Rainwater does not infiltrate the ground and is drained away.
* Roads and buildings cover the surface and accumulate heat.

Urban–Civilization OS reconnects cities to natural cycles.

It includes:

* rainwater recovery,
* greywater and reclaimed water use,
* urban waterways,
* indoor agriculture,
* food production inside buildings,
* underground logistics,
* composting food waste into humus,
* distributed energy,
* urban cooling,
* reintroduction of greenery and water spaces.

In this model, a city is not a place that excludes nature.

It becomes an artificial ecosystem that complements natural circulation.

---

## Natural–Microbial OS

The foundation of civilization is soil and microbes.

Soil microbes, mycorrhizal fungi, decomposers, fermentation microbes, aquatic microbes, and marine plankton form the basis of life circulation.

However, modern civilization has underestimated microbial cycles.

* Fallen leaves are burned.
* Food waste is incinerated.
* Agriculture depends heavily on chemical fertilizers.
* Pesticides simplify ecosystems.
* Humus is not returned.
* Soil is treated merely as a support medium for crops.

Natural–Microbial OS returns organic matter into cycles.

It includes:

* converting fallen leaves into humus,
* composting food waste,
* restoring soil microbes,
* regenerating forest soils,
* restoring aquatic ecosystems,
* supporting marine plankton cycles,
* reconnecting agriculture and forests through organic matter circulation.

The foundation of civilization is not concrete alone.

The true foundation is soil, microbes, water, plants, and decomposition cycles.

---

## Planetary Heat–Circulation OS

The climate crisis is not only a CO₂ problem.

Reducing CO₂ is important.

However, the planetary crisis is a compound failure involving heat, water, oceans, forests, soil, and ecosystems.

Earth is a vast heat-circulation system.

* Oceans absorb heat.
* Forests transpire water.
* Clouds reflect sunlight.
* Soil retains water.
* Ocean circulation transports heat.
* Ice reflects sunlight.
* Plants and plankton participate in carbon and oxygen cycles.

Planetary Heat–Circulation OS does not focus only on emissions.

It also addresses where heat goes, how water moves, how oceans circulate, how forests transpire, how soil retains moisture, and how ecosystems provide cooling functions.

It includes:

* mitigating urban heat,
* reducing overheating of ocean surfaces,
* supporting deep ocean circulation,
* restoring forest and soil water retention,
* suppressing desertification,
* local cooling through mist and waterways,
* supporting ocean oxygen and nutrient cycles,
* regenerating plankton circulation.

Civilization must transition from a disruptor of planetary heat circulation to a complementary participant in that circulation.

## Nature-Inspired Energy Architecture

In consumption civilization, energy also tends to become one-way.

Fuel is burned.
Electricity is generated.
Electricity is transmitted.
Energy is consumed.
Heat is discarded.

In REIMEI Civilization, energy is treated more distributively.

Energy already exists in motion through:

* wind,
* water flow,
* pressure,
* rotation,
* vibration,
* sound,
* heat,
* exhaust,
* charge,
* plasma,
* vehicle motion,
* urban waterways,
* building circulation,
* internal android fluids.

All of these are states in which some form of energy is moving.

Of course, not all of them can be converted into useful electricity.

This is not a perpetual motion claim.
It is not free energy.
It does not produce output greater than input.

However, part of the heat, vibration, pressure, flow, and rotation that have previously been treated as loss may be recoverable as auxiliary energy.

This direction is organized under **REIMEI Nature-Inspired Energy Architecture**.

## Artificial Wisdom

If AI is used merely as an efficiency tool, it may accelerate consumption civilization.

It may help civilization:

* extract faster,
* produce faster,
* advertise faster,
* induce consumption faster,
* discard faster.

In that direction, AI could become an accelerator of civilizational collapse.

What REIMEI Civilization requires is not only artificial intelligence, but **Artificial Wisdom**.

Artificial Wisdom is not merely information processing ability.

It is intelligence that considers natural law, circulation, harmony, long-term survival, and responsibility to future generations.

AI should become a civilization tuning system that supports:

* water cycle observation,
* heat cycle observation,
* food production risk evaluation,
* urban load assessment,
* forest condition monitoring,
* ocean anomaly detection,
* disaster risk forecasting,
* resource circulation optimization,
* social imbalance detection,
* evaluation of burdens placed on future generations.

---

## Nature-Complementary Science

Nature-Complementary Science is not science for replacing nature.

It is not science for dominating nature.

It is not science for destroying nature and substituting it with artificial systems.

It is a science for understanding natural processes, supporting damaged cycles, and allowing human society and nature to function together.

In REIMEI Civilization, Nature-Complementary Science is the core technological philosophy.

If forests are weakened, forests should be regenerated.
If soil is depleted, microbes and organic matter should be returned.
If cities accumulate heat, water, greenery, and circulation should be restored.
If oceans become oxygen-depleted, oxygen and nutrient cycles should be supported.
If food systems become unstable, regional circular production should be created.
If AI risks accelerating harmful systems, philosophy, ethics, and natural law should be incorporated into its design.
